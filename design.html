Design Registration & Protection

Protect the unique visual appearance of your products with comprehensive industrial design registration services in India and internationally.

What is a Registered Design?

An industrial design refers to the ornamental or aesthetic aspects of a product. Design registration protects the unique visual features – shape, configuration, pattern, ornamentation, composition of lines or colours – that give a product its distinctive appearance and appeal.

In India, design protection is governed by the Designs Act, 2000 and Designs Rules, 2001. A registered design gives the owner the exclusive right to apply the design to any article in the class in which the design is registered, preventing others from copying or substantially imitating the registered design.

What Designs Can Be Registered?

  • Product shapes, configurations, and contours
  • Surface patterns, ornamentation, and decorations
  • Colour combinations as applied to products
  • Packaging and container designs
  • Textile, fabric, and surface designs
  • Digital icons and graphical user interfaces
  • Furniture and interior design elements
  • Jewellery and fashion designs

Design vs. Other IP Rights

  • Design Registration: Protects the visual appearance of products – fastest and most cost-effective protection for product aesthetics
  • Copyright: Automatically protects artistic works, overlaps with design in some cases for artistic elements
  • Trademark: Protects brand identity – shape marks can complement design protection
  • Patent: Protects functional inventions – design patents protect ornamental aspects of utility items

Benefits of Design Registration

  • Exclusive right to use the design commercially
  • Protection against copying, imitation, and counterfeiting
  • Enhanced commercial value and competitive advantage
  • Foundation for international design protection
  • Right to initiate civil and criminal proceedings against infringers
  • Licensing and franchising opportunities

Design Registration Process

1

Novelty Search

We conduct a thorough search to ensure your design is novel and not already protected by existing registered designs.

2

Representation Preparation

We prepare professional drawings and representations of the design from all required views, as per registry requirements.

3

Application Filing

We file the design application with the Patent Office, India in the appropriate class of the Locarno Classification.

4

Examination

The registry examines the application for compliance and novelty. We respond to any objections or deficiencies raised.

5

Registration

Upon successful examination, the design is registered and a certificate issued. Registration is valid for 10 years, extendable by 5 years.

Key Facts

  • Protection: 10 years + 5 year extension
  • Registration time: 6–12 months
  • Classes: Locarno Classification
  • International: Hague System (90+ countries)
  • Novelty requirement: New and original

Documents Required

  • Design drawings/photographs (all views)
  • Description of the design
  • Priority documents (if claiming priority)
  • Power of Attorney
  • Statement of novelty
  • Locarno class identification
