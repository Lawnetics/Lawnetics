Trademark Registration & Protection

Comprehensive trademark services to protect your brand identity across India and international markets.

What is a Trademark?

A trademark is any distinctive sign, symbol, word, phrase, logo, design, or combination thereof that identifies and distinguishes the goods or services of one enterprise from those of another. Trademarks are invaluable business assets that build brand recognition, consumer trust, and commercial value over time.

In India, trademarks are governed by the Trade Marks Act, 1999 and Trade Marks Rules, 2017. Registration grants the owner exclusive rights to use the mark in connection with the goods/services for which it is registered, and provides legal recourse against infringers.

Why Register Your Trademark?

Trademark registration provides your business with critical advantages:

  • Exclusive legal right to use your mark in your business category
  • Legal presumption of ownership and right to use nationally
  • Ability to sue infringers and recover damages
  • Public notice preventing others from adopting confusingly similar marks
  • Foundation for international trademark protection
  • Valuable business asset that can be licensed, franchised or sold
  • Protection against counterfeiting and brand dilution

Types of Marks We Register

  • Word marks – brand names, slogans, taglines
  • Device marks – logos, emblems, symbols
  • Composite marks – combination of words and device
  • Colour marks – distinctive colours or colour combinations
  • Shape marks – distinctive product or packaging shapes
  • Sound marks – distinctive audio signatures
  • Collective marks – marks used by associations of enterprises
  • Certification marks – marks indicating compliance with a standard

Trademark Registration Process

1

Trademark Search

We conduct thorough searches of the Trade Marks Registry database and common law sources to assess registrability and identify potential conflicts.

2

Application Filing

We prepare and file the trademark application with the Trade Marks Registry in the appropriate class(es) of goods/services.

3

Examination

The Registry examines the application and issues an examination report. We respond to any objections raised by the Examiner.

4

Advertisement

If accepted, the mark is advertised in the Trade Marks Journal, open to opposition by third parties for four months.

5

Registration

If no opposition is filed or opposition is decided in your favour, the mark is registered and a certificate issued. Registration is valid for 10 years, renewable indefinitely.

Quick Facts

  • Protection period: 10 years (renewable)
  • Registration time: 12–24 months typically
  • Classes available: 45 (Nice Classification)
  • International: 180+ countries via Madrid
  • Symbol: ® after registration, ™ pending

Documents Required

  • Name and address of applicant
  • Representation of the mark/logo
  • List of goods/services
  • Date of first use (if applicable)
  • Power of Attorney (signed)
  • User affidavit (if claiming prior use)
