Trademark Registration & Protection

Comprehensive trademark services to protect your brand identity across India and international markets.

What is a Trademark?

A trademark is any distinctive sign, symbol, word, phrase, logo, design, or combination thereof that identifies and distinguishes the goods or services of one enterprise from those of another. Trademarks are invaluable business assets that build brand recognition, consumer trust, and commercial value over time.

In India, trademarks are governed by the Trade Marks Act, 1999 and Trade Marks Rules, 2017. Registration grants the owner exclusive rights to use the mark in connection with the goods/services for which it is registered, and provides legal recourse against infringers.

Our Trademark Services

  • Comprehensive trademark search and availability analysis
  • Trademark application filing with the Trade Marks Registry, India
  • International trademark registration via the Madrid Protocol (180+ countries)
  • Trademark opposition and hearing representation
  • Trademark renewal and portfolio management
  • Well-known trademark status applications
  • Assignment and licensing of trademark rights
  • Trademark watch services and monitoring
  • Infringement notices, cease & desist letters
  • Domain name disputes (UDRP proceedings)

Why Register Your Trademark?

Trademark registration provides your business with critical advantages:

  • Exclusive legal right to use your mark in your business category
  • Legal presumption of ownership and right to use nationally
  • Ability to sue infringers and recover damages
  • Public notice preventing others from adopting confusingly similar marks
  • Foundation for international trademark protection
  • Valuable business asset that can be licensed, franchised or sold
  • Protection against counterfeiting and brand dilution

Types of Marks We Register

  • Word marks – brand names, slogans, taglines
  • Device marks – logos, emblems, symbols
  • Composite marks – combination of words and device
  • Colour marks – distinctive colours or colour combinations
  • Shape marks – distinctive product or packaging shapes
  • Sound marks – distinctive audio signatures
  • Collective marks – marks used by associations of enterprises
  • Certification marks – marks indicating compliance with a standard

Trademark Registration Process

1

Trademark Search

We conduct thorough searches of the Trade Marks Registry database and common law sources to assess registrability and identify potential conflicts.

2

Application Filing

We prepare and file the trademark application with the Trade Marks Registry in the appropriate class(es) of goods/services.

3

Examination

The Registry examines the application and issues an examination report. We respond to any objections raised by the Examiner.

4

Advertisement

If accepted, the mark is advertised in the Trade Marks Journal, open to opposition by third parties for four months.

5

Registration

If no opposition is filed or opposition is decided in your favour, the mark is registered and a certificate issued. Registration is valid for 10 years, renewable indefinitely.

Quick Facts

  • Protection period: 10 years (renewable)
  • Registration time: 12–24 months typically
  • Classes available: 45 (Nice Classification)
  • International: 180+ countries via Madrid
  • Symbol: ® after registration, ™ pending

Documents Required

  • Name and address of applicant
  • Representation of the mark/logo
  • List of goods/services
  • Date of first use (if applicable)
  • Power of Attorney (signed)
  • User affidavit (if claiming prior use)

Copyright Registration & Protection

Protect your original creative works – literary, artistic, musical, cinematic and software – with comprehensive copyright services.

What is Copyright?

Copyright is a form of intellectual property protection granted to the creators of original literary, artistic, dramatic, musical, and other creative works. In India, copyright protection arises automatically upon creation of an original work, but registration provides significant evidentiary and legal advantages in enforcement.

Copyright in India is governed by the Copyright Act, 1957 (as amended). It gives creators exclusive rights to reproduce, distribute, perform, display, and create derivative works from their original creations for a specified period – generally the lifetime of the author plus 60 years.

Works Eligible for Copyright Protection

  • Literary works – books, articles, novels, poetry, scripts
  • Computer programs and software codes
  • Dramatic works – plays, screenplays, choreography
  • Musical works – compositions, lyrics
  • Artistic works – paintings, drawings, photographs, sculptures
  • Cinematograph films and sound recordings
  • Databases and compilations
  • Architectural works and blueprints
  • Multimedia works and digital content

Our Copyright Services

  • Copyright registration with the Copyright Office, India
  • Copyright clearance searches and due diligence
  • Drafting of copyright assignment and licensing agreements
  • Copyright infringement analysis and opinions
  • Cease & desist notices for copyright infringement
  • Copyright enforcement and litigation support
  • Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA) takedowns
  • Software and IT copyright protection strategies
  • Creative industry advisory services

Benefits of Copyright Registration

  • Creates a public record of ownership of the work
  • Provides prima facie evidence of validity in court proceedings
  • Enables filing of infringement suits in courts
  • Allows recovery of statutory damages and attorney's fees
  • Protects against plagiarism and unauthorized reproduction
  • Facilitates licensing and commercialization of creative works
  • Provides a foundation for international copyright protection

Copyright Registration Process

1

Work Assessment

We review your work to confirm its eligibility for copyright protection and advise on the best strategy for registration and protection.

2

Application Preparation

3

4

Mandatory Wait Period

The Copyright Office issues a 30-day waiting notice for third parties to raise any objections to the registration.

5

Certificate Issuance

After the waiting period and examination, the Copyright Office issues the registration certificate, typically within 6–12 months.

Copyright Duration

  • Literary/Artistic: Life + 60 years
  • Cinematograph Films: 60 years
  • Sound Recordings: 60 years
  • Photographs: Life + 60 years
  • Government works: 60 years

Documents Required

  • Copies of the work (as applicable)
  • Author/owner details
  • Nature and description of work
  • Date of creation/publication
  • Power of Attorney
  • NOC from co-authors (if any)

Design Registration & Protection

Protect the unique visual appearance of your products with comprehensive industrial design registration services in India and internationally.

What is a Registered Design?

An industrial design refers to the ornamental or aesthetic aspects of a product. Design registration protects the unique visual features – shape, configuration, pattern, ornamentation, composition of lines or colours – that give a product its distinctive appearance and appeal.

In India, design protection is governed by the Designs Act, 2000 and Designs Rules, 2001. A registered design gives the owner the exclusive right to apply the design to any article in the class in which the design is registered, preventing others from copying or substantially imitating the registered design.

Our Design Registration Services

  • Design novelty search and prior art analysis
  • Industrial design application filing with the Patent Office, India
  • International design registration (Hague System)
  • Design prosecution and examination response
  • Design portfolio management and renewal
  • Design infringement analysis and enforcement
  • Assignment and licensing of design rights
  • Design watch and monitoring services

What Designs Can Be Registered?

  • Product shapes, configurations, and contours
  • Surface patterns, ornamentation, and decorations
  • Colour combinations as applied to products
  • Packaging and container designs
  • Textile, fabric, and surface designs
  • Digital icons and graphical user interfaces
  • Furniture and interior design elements
  • Jewellery and fashion designs

Design vs. Other IP Rights

  • Design Registration: Protects the visual appearance of products – fastest and most cost-effective protection for product aesthetics
  • Copyright: Automatically protects artistic works, overlaps with design in some cases for artistic elements
  • Trademark: Protects brand identity – shape marks can complement design protection
  • Patent: Protects functional inventions – design patents protect ornamental aspects of utility items

Benefits of Design Registration

  • Exclusive right to use the design commercially
  • Protection against copying, imitation, and counterfeiting
  • Enhanced commercial value and competitive advantage
  • Foundation for international design protection
  • Right to initiate civil and criminal proceedings against infringers
  • Licensing and franchising opportunities

  • Registration time: 6–12 months
  • Classes: Locarno Classification
  • International: Hague System (90+ countries)
  • Novelty requirement: New and original

Documents Required

Patent Registration & Management

Transform your inventions into protected, commercially valuable assets with our comprehensive patent services across India and globally.

What is a Patent?

A patent is an exclusive right granted for an invention – a product or process that provides a new way of doing something, or offers a new technical solution to a problem. In exchange for full public disclosure of the invention, the patent owner receives the right to exclude others from making, using, selling, or importing the patented invention for the duration of the patent term.

In India, patents are governed by the Patents Act, 1970 (as amended). A patent is granted for 20 years from the filing date, subject to annual renewal fees. India is a signatory to the Patent Cooperation Treaty (PCT), enabling international patent protection through a single application process.

Types of Patents

  • Utility Patents: New and useful processes, machines, articles of manufacture, or compositions of matter
  • Product Patents: New and inventive products or substances
  • Process Patents: New and inventive methods or processes for making or using a product
  • Biotechnology Patents: Microorganisms, biological materials, and related processes
  • Computer-Related Inventions: Software with technical applications and hardware inventions

Our Patent Services

  • Patentability searches and opinions
  • Patent drafting and specification preparation
  • Provisional and complete specification filing
  • PCT (International) patent applications
  • National phase entry in multiple countries
  • Patent prosecution and examination response
  • Patent portfolio management and maintenance
  • Freedom-to-operate opinions and risk analysis
  • Patent valuation and licensing strategies
  • Prior art searches and patent landscapes
  • Patent invalidation and opposition proceedings
  • Technology transfer agreements

Requirements for Patentability

  • Novelty: The invention must be new and not disclosed anywhere in the world prior to the filing date
  • Inventive Step: The invention must not be obvious to a person skilled in the relevant field
  • Industrial Applicability: The invention must be capable of being made or used in some kind of industry
  • Not Excluded: The invention must not fall under the categories excluded from patentability under Indian law

Patent Registration Process

1

Invention Disclosure & Patentability Search

We conduct a thorough prior art search to assess novelty and inventive step, and advise on patentability prospects.

2

Patent Drafting

Our expert patent attorneys draft claims, description, abstract, and drawings to maximize protection scope and enforce-ability.

3

Provisional/Complete Filing

We file the provisional application to establish a priority date, followed by the complete specification within 12 months, or directly file the complete specification.

4

Publication & Examination

The application is published 18 months from filing. We file a Request for Examination (RFE) and respond to First Examination Reports (FER).

5

Grant & Maintenance

Upon successful prosecution, the patent is granted and published. We manage annual renewal filings to maintain the patent in force for up to 20 years.

Key Facts

  • Protection term: 20 years from filing
  • Annual renewal: After grant
  • PCT: 150+ member countries
  • Priority period: 12 months
  • Publication: 18 months from filing

What Cannot Be Patented

  • Discoveries, scientific theories
  • Mathematical methods
  • Mental acts, games, business methods
  • Presentation of information
  • Plants, animals (except microorganisms)
  • Traditional knowledge

Geographical Indications Registration

Protect region-specific products and their unique qualities with comprehensive Geographical Indication services.

What is a Geographical Indication (GI)?

A Geographical Indication (GI) is an indication used on goods that have a specific geographical origin and possess qualities, reputation, or characteristics that are essentially attributable to that place of origin. GIs serve as a mark of authenticity and quality assurance for consumers, while protecting producers' rights and preserving traditional knowledge and cultural heritage.

In India, GIs are governed by the Geographical Indications of Goods (Registration and Protection) Act, 1999. Well-known Indian GIs include Darjeeling Tea, Kancheepuram Silk, Kolhapuri Chappal, Basmati Rice, Alphonso Mango, and Tirupati Laddu, among many others.

Our GI Services

  • GI eligibility assessment and strategy advisory
  • GI application preparation and filing
  • GI prosecution and examination support
  • Opposition and hearing representation before GI Registry
  • Authorized user registration for GI products
  • GI enforcement and infringement actions
  • International GI protection strategies
  • Advisory to GI producer associations and cooperatives
  • Certification and quality standard advisory

Who Can Apply for a GI?

  • Any association of producers or organizations representing producers
  • Government authorities (Central or State)
  • Competent authorities or any authority established by or under any law
  • Producers of goods with specific geographical characteristics

Benefits of GI Registration

  • Legal protection of the GI from unauthorized use by third parties
  • Protection of the name and reputation of genuine products
  • Premium pricing and enhanced market access for GI producers
  • Preservation of traditional knowledge and cultural heritage
  • Export promotion and international market recognition
  • Foundation for international GI protection (WTO TRIPS, bilateral agreements)
  • Tool against counterfeiting and fraudulent imitation of GI products

Key Facts

  • Protection period: 10 years (renewable)
  • Registry: GI Registry, Chennai
  • Indian GIs: 400+
  • WTO TRIPS: International recognition
  • Authorized users: Registered separately

Documents Required

  • Statement of case and proof of origin
  • Standards of quality and production
  • Map of geographical area
  • Details of producer association
  • Evidence of historical use
  • Power of Attorney

Trade Secret Protection & Advisory

Safeguard your confidential business information, formulas, processes, and proprietary data with robust trade secret strategies.

What are Trade Secrets?

A trade secret is any confidential business information that provides a competitive advantage and is subject to reasonable steps to keep it secret. Unlike patents, trademarks, or copyrights, trade secrets are protected without registration – as long as the information remains confidential and has commercial value because of its secrecy.

Examples of trade secrets include formulas (like the Coca-Cola recipe), manufacturing processes, client databases, business strategies, software algorithms, and any other confidential commercial information. While India does not have a dedicated trade secret law, protection is available through contract law, tort law, and equitable remedies.

Our Trade Secret Services

  • Trade secret audit and identification
  • Trade secret protection strategy and policy development
  • Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) drafting and review
  • Employee confidentiality agreements and restrictive covenants
  • Trade secret misappropriation advisory and legal action
  • Internal IP policies and compliance procedures
  • IP due diligence for mergers, acquisitions, and investments
  • Technology transfer and licensing agreements
  • Legal action for breach of confidence and misappropriation

Key Elements of Trade Secret Protection

  • Secrecy: The information must not be generally known or easily ascertainable by the public or competitors
  • Commercial Value: The information must have economic value derived from its secrecy
  • Reasonable Measures: The owner must take reasonable steps to maintain the information's secrecy
  • Documentation: Proper records of what constitutes the trade secret and when it was created

Why Trade Secret Protection Matters

  • No registration required – protection begins immediately
  • Protection can last indefinitely as long as secrecy is maintained
  • Covers information that may not qualify for patent protection
  • Cost-effective alternative or complement to patent protection
  • Protects against industrial espionage and employee misappropriation
  • Critical for technology companies, pharmaceuticals, and consumer goods

Common Trade Secrets

  • Chemical formulas and compositions
  • Manufacturing processes
  • Customer and supplier lists
  • Business strategies and plans
  • Software source code
  • Pricing and financial data
  • Marketing strategies

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly can I get an injunction to stop IP infringement?

In urgent cases of IP infringement, we can file an application for an ex-parte interim injunction – the court may grant temporary relief within 24–48 hours of filing, without even hearing the other party. We have successfully obtained such urgent injunctions in cases of trademark infringement, copyright piracy, and other IP violations.

What is a John Doe order and how can it help?

A John Doe order (known as "Ashok Kumar" order in India) is an injunction granted against unknown defendants – particularly useful in cases of online piracy and large-scale counterfeiting where infringers' identities are unknown. It allows action against websites, ISPs, and distributors of infringing content even before identifying the specific infringers.

What damages can I claim in an IP infringement case?

In IP infringement cases, you can seek: (a) Compensatory damages – actual losses suffered due to infringement; (b) Account of profits – the infringer's profits attributable to the infringement; (c) Punitive damages – in cases of flagrant infringement; (d) Legal costs; (e) Delivery up and destruction of infringing goods. The appropriate remedy depends on the nature of the case.

Can LawNetics help if someone is falsely accusing me of IP infringement?

Absolutely. We provide vigorous defence against unjustified IP claims. This may include: challenging the validity of the opponent's IP rights, demonstrating that your activities do not constitute infringement (non-infringement opinions), filing for cancellation/revocation of the opponent's IP, and seeking damages for groundless threats of infringement proceedings.

International IP Tools

International IP Filing Systems

🌐

Madrid System (Trademarks)

File a single international trademark application through WIPO to seek protection in up to 130 member countries. Cost-effective way to build international trademark protection from a single India-based application.

💡

PCT (Patents)

The Patent Cooperation Treaty allows filing of a single international patent application that is effective in 150+ member countries. Provides 30 months from priority date to enter national phases, enabling cost-effective international patent strategy.

🎨

Hague System (Designs)

Register industrial designs in multiple countries through a single international application filed with WIPO. Currently covers 90+ countries and regional design offices, including the EU and USA.
