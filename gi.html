Geographical Indications Registration

Protect region-specific products and their unique qualities with comprehensive Geographical Indication services.

What is a Geographical Indication (GI)?

A Geographical Indication (GI) is an indication used on goods that have a specific geographical origin and possess qualities, reputation, or characteristics that are essentially attributable to that place of origin. GIs serve as a mark of authenticity and quality assurance for consumers, while protecting producers' rights and preserving traditional knowledge and cultural heritage.

In India, GIs are governed by the Geographical Indications of Goods (Registration and Protection) Act, 1999. Well-known Indian GIs include Darjeeling Tea, Kancheepuram Silk, Kolhapuri Chappal, Basmati Rice, Alphonso Mango, and Tirupati Laddu, among many others.

Who Can Apply for a GI?

  • Any association of producers or organizations representing producers
  • Government authorities (Central or State)
  • Competent authorities or any authority established by or under any law
  • Producers of goods with specific geographical characteristics

Benefits of GI Registration

  • Legal protection of the GI from unauthorized use by third parties
  • Protection of the name and reputation of genuine products
  • Premium pricing and enhanced market access for GI producers
  • Preservation of traditional knowledge and cultural heritage
  • Export promotion and international market recognition
  • Foundation for international GI protection (WTO TRIPS, bilateral agreements)
  • Tool against counterfeiting and fraudulent imitation of GI products

Key Facts

  • Protection period: 10 years (renewable)
  • Registry: GI Registry, Chennai
  • Indian GIs: 400+
  • WTO TRIPS: International recognition
  • Authorized users: Registered separately

Documents Required

  • Statement of case and proof of origin
  • Standards of quality and production
  • Map of geographical area
  • Details of producer association
  • Evidence of historical use
  • Power of Attorney
