Copyright Registration & Protection

Protect your original creative works – literary, artistic, musical, cinematic and software – with comprehensive copyright services.

What is Copyright?

Copyright is a form of intellectual property protection granted to the creators of original literary, artistic, dramatic, musical, and other creative works. In India, copyright protection arises automatically upon creation of an original work, but registration provides significant evidentiary and legal advantages in enforcement.

Copyright in India is governed by the Copyright Act, 1957 (as amended). It gives creators exclusive rights to reproduce, distribute, perform, display, and create derivative works from their original creations for a specified period – generally the lifetime of the author plus 60 years.

Works Eligible for Copyright Protection

  • Literary works – books, articles, novels, poetry, scripts
  • Computer programs and software codes
  • Dramatic works – plays, screenplays, choreography
  • Musical works – compositions, lyrics
  • Artistic works – paintings, drawings, photographs, sculptures
  • Cinematograph films and sound recordings
  • Databases and compilations
  • Architectural works and blueprints
  • Multimedia works and digital content

Benefits of Copyright Registration

  • Creates a public record of ownership of the work
  • Provides prima facie evidence of validity in court proceedings
  • Enables filing of infringement suits in courts
  • Allows recovery of statutory damages and attorney's fees
  • Protects against plagiarism and unauthorized reproduction
  • Facilitates licensing and commercialization of creative works
  • Provides a foundation for international copyright protection

Copyright Registration Process

1

Work Assessment

We review your work to confirm its eligibility for copyright protection and advise on the best strategy for registration and protection.

2

Application Preparation

We prepare the copyright application, including all required statements, declarations, and supporting documents.

3

Filing & Diary Number

We file the application with the Copyright Office and obtain a diary number, which is issued on the same day of filing.

4

Mandatory Wait Period

The Copyright Office issues a 30-day waiting notice for third parties to raise any objections to the registration.

5

Certificate Issuance

After the waiting period and examination, the Copyright Office issues the registration certificate, typically within 6–12 months.

Copyright Duration

  • Literary/Artistic: Life + 60 years
  • Cinematograph Films: 60 years
  • Sound Recordings: 60 years
  • Photographs: Life + 60 years
  • Government works: 60 years

Documents Required

  • Copies of the work (as applicable)
  • Author/owner details
  • Nature and description of work
  • Date of creation/publication
  • Power of Attorney
  • NOC from co-authors (if any)
